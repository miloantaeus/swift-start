# AI Freelancer Client Qualification Scorecard

**Score any lead in 2 minutes. Spend your time only on winners.**

---

## The 5-Factor Scoring System

Rate each lead 1–5 on each factor. Total out of 25.

| Factor | What to Look For | Score |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| **1. Problem Clarity** | Do they have a specific, describable problem you can actually solve? | __/5 |
| **2. Budget** | Have they mentioned a budget or can you ask ("What's range for this?")? | __/5 |
| **3. Timeline** | Is there a real deadline — this week, this month, or "asap"? | __/5 |
| **4. Decision Maker** | Are they the decision-maker, or do they need to report upward? | __/5 |
| **5. Warmth** | Referral, mutual connection, or have you thoroughly researched their situation? | __/5 |
| **TOTAL** | | **__/25** |

---

## How to Read Your Score

| Score | Decision | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **18–25** | **TOP PRIORITY** | Reply within 2 hours. Send a tailored pitch immediately. |
| **12–17** | **SCHEDULE A CALL** | Reply within 24 hours. Propose a 15-min discovery call. |
| **Below 12** | **NURTURE ONLY** | Add to email sequence. Don't chase. |

---

## The Budget Question — How to Ask Without Sounding Greedy

> *"To make sure we're a good fit, what's the budget range you're working with for this?"*

If they deflect:
> *"No worries — I find projects typically range from $X to $Y depending on scope. Does that align with what you're thinking?"*

---

## The Decision-Maker Checklist

Ask yourself:
- [ ] Have they said "I can approve this" or "I just need to run it by my manager"?
- [ ] Are they the one signing the contract (or do you need to loop in someone else)?
- [ ] If they need approval, can you make the case compelling enough for them to champion it?

**Red flag:** Anyone who needs to "check with the team" but can't give you a timeline = low priority.

---

## Quick Research Checklist (2 minutes)

Before scoring, verify:
- [ ] LinkedIn: What do they do? What's their company's problem right now?
- [ ] Recent posts/articles: What are they talking about publicly?
- [ ] Job postings: What roles are they hiring? (Reveals pain points.)
- [ ] News: Any recent funding, expansion, restructure? (Creates urgency.)

## The Scorecard in Practice

**Example A — Score 22/25:**
> *SaaS founder, $500–$2K/mo budget, wants AI客服 implemented in 2 weeks, decision-maker, found via warm LinkedIn intro.*
> → **Reply in 1 hour. Propose scope + terms.**

**Example B — Score 13/25:**
> *Inquired about content writing, vague timeline, not sure of budget, reached out cold via website contact form.*
> → **Reply in 24 hours. Schedule a 15-min call to qualify further before scoping.**

**Example C — Score 8/25:**
> *"We're looking for someone to maybe help with some stuff. Not sure of budget yet."*
> → **Nurture sequence. Don't spend more than a polite acknowledgment.**

---

## Bonus: The 3-Question Qualification Call Script

If you get them on the phone, ask these 3 in order:

1. **"Walk me through what you're trying to accomplish?"** — Tests problem clarity and their readiness
2. **"What's the timeline looking like?"** — Separates serious buyers from browsers
3. **"What range are you comfortable investing to get this done?"** — Removes the awkward money conversation

If all 3 land well → send a proposal within 24 hours.
